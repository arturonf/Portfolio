Projects

Thesis: A Grasp-Tabu Heuristic for the Handover Minimization Problem

May 2020

This thesis proposes a hybrid GRASP-Tabu heuristic that finds feasible solutions for the NP-Hard Capacitated Clustering Problem (CCP). The problem focuses on minimizing the handover in telecommunications grids.

Electric Scooter

May 2019

Final joint project for electrical machines and power electronics subjects in which we designed and built both the mechanical and electrical parts of an electric scooter with velocity control using a boost converter and a 500W dc Motor.

Line Follower with PID

Nov 2018

Final Control System's Project. We designed a line follower with 8 sensors and PID control for competitions. PID (proportional integral derivative) controllers use a control loop feedback mechanism to control process variables and are the most accurate and stable controller.

XXIX International Industrial and Logistics Engineering Congress

Apr 2018

Required activity for the student council. Being the event's coordinator in the Directive Board of Industrial Engineering, I was in charge of the planning and logistics involved in the XXIX International Industrial and Logistics Engineering Congress hosted at UDLAP. We had 3 days of lectures and workshops about different relevant topics and 5 trips to plants such as Bimbo, Yakult, Schaeffler, BBVA, and Stanley.

Robotic Vacuum Cleaner

Nov 2017

Final instrumentation and signal processing project. We designed a self-steered robotic vacuum cleaner that with the input of 3 ultrasonic sensors was aware of objects and walls, thus changing its direction when needed.

Natural interfaces for navigation based on head movement recognition

Nov 2015

Assisted on the Master's Degree thesis 'Natural interfaces for navigation based on head movement recognition'. The thesis presents 'Head Gesture Control', a natural interface alternative to traditional control peripherals to navigate virtual spaces by detecting movements that the user makes with their head. Among my tasks was designing the user experience test and data analysis, as well as movement calibration and head recognition using the Viola-Jones algorithm.

Self Medicating in Puebla

Apr 2014

In this research project conducted for an extra course in high school, we analyzed the percentage of people who self-medicate in Puebla and we explored the consequences that this behavior can bring.

Social Network Impact on Grades

Apr 2014

Graduation project conducted during my last high school year. We gathered grade information about students who spend more than average time on social networks and a control group (people who use around average time on social networks). Then we analyzed the results to see if there was a correlation between grades and time spent on social networks.
